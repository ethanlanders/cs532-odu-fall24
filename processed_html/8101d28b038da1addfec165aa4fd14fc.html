Facebook Twitter Philippines indigenous leader Victoria Tauli-Corpuz is not afraid of a fight. As a young woman in the 1970s, she joined the movement to protect her mountain homeland from major dam and logging projects. Her home was raided during martial law a decade later, but she insists it was all “fine.” But now Ms. Tauli-Corpuz, who is the UN Special Rapporteur on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples, finds herself on the frontline of a new battle. Earlier this year, the Philippine authorities included her name on a list of people declared as “communist terrorists”. Audio
